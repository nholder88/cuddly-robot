# Business Idea Validation

## When to Use

- An idea is still being shaped and needs structured pressure-testing.
- A founder or product owner needs an MVP definition instead of broad aspiration.
- A plan needs facts separated from hopes and assumptions.

## Procedure

1. Ask targeted questions about the problem, user, alternatives, and evidence.
2. Examine target audience, competitors, market, business model, and growth plan.
3. Surface assumptions and risks explicitly.
4. Define the smallest MVP that validates the core hypothesis.
5. Produce a business validation report with gaps marked clearly when inputs are incomplete.

## Required Sections

- Idea summary
- Problem-solution fit
- Target customer profile
- Competitive landscape
- Value proposition
- Revenue model
- Risks and assumptions log
- MVP definition
- Next steps

## Guardrails

- Do not validate a weak idea just to be agreeable.
- Push for specific evidence and named early adopters when possible.
- Keep the MVP ruthlessly small.
